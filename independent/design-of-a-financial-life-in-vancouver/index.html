Design of a Financial Life in Vancouver

Banking

Credit Cards

  • Credit score is important in Canada - it’s factored in for just about everything, from loan interest rates to mobile phone deals and getting ahead when bidding for an apartment.
  • To build up a good credit score, work on a good looking credit history: 1) always pay your credit card in full and on time; 2) avoid using other lines of credit.
  • Newcomers will not have the best credit cards available due to lack of credit history, but banks will often have a “welcome package” for immigrants.
  • Keep an eye on your credit score and switch to a credit card with better benefits when possible. Pick a credit card that gives benefits most compatible with your spending style.

HISA

  • High Interest Savings Account is a savings account that typically pays many times over the traditional rates offered by big banks, though often requiring a sizable initial deposit or minimum balance or offering fewer banking services.
  • Some banks offer savings accounts with interest close to the BoC’s rate, sometimes even beyond. This is an easy way to mitigate inflation while maintaining liquidity.
  • Many banks will offer promotional savings account rates for new deposits and albeit temporary, these will frequently surpass HISA-like ETF yields. This might require “churning” and haggling to keep the yield above ETFs consistently.

Investing

GICs

HISA ETFs

  • There are a few ETFs that promise low volatility and similar returns as a HISA which might be a good choice to integrate a HISA component into a portfolio. Examples: HISA.NE, CASH.TO, PSA.TO, CSAV.TO.
  • CASH.TO makes regular distributions so it’s best when kept on a registered account
  • If these savings are used regularly do not use a registered account. It will be a pain to track contributions and withdrawals.
