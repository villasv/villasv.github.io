    Bugonia, 6 out of 10
    A different kind of suspense and thriller, definitely current given the rise of conspiracy theorists in latest decades. The overall acting and directing was good, as unnervingly as probably intended.

    The ending… no way it’s supposed to be taken too literally, after all there’s zero chance that Teddy got anything right; but I can’t help but feel like it was a potential not fully realized. Would making it something obvious and straightforward too boring? Maybe. Maybe still better than what we got. Sometimes a fade to black is better than ending with the bang of a clown gun.
