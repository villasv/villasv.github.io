Aperture

The aperture is the diameter of the opening that allows light into the camera. A wider or narrower opening changes the amount of light that passes through during the time the shutter is open.

Because a wider opening allows light to come in more diverse angles, a bigger aperture also decreases the Depth of Field. Small apertures yield the largest depths of field.

Very small apertures introduce might introduce light diffraction.

Large apertures also increase the effect of lens aberrations.
