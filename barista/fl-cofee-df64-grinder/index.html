FL Cofee DF64 Grinder

Released at about 500~600 USD. Flat burrs. Can use different burrs like the SSP.

Grinding at 5 for Espresso. Around 50-55 for a V60 pour-over.

Reviews

Notes on James Hoffman’s Review

It has a bit of retention due to a cheap silicon de-clumping screen.
There are mods to replace this screen that you can buy out there.
Crazy chaff static if you don’t spritz the beans (RDT).
If you grind around 30 g or more, the default cup is more than half full.
Then when you pump the bellow, it might blow off ground coffee out of the cup.
Changing and cleaning the burrs is easy, as well as the chamber.
It doesn’t look as nice, the ergonomics are a bit questionable. The power button is weirdly placed.
And what’s up with that font for the numbers?
The bellow is cheap, but a necessity because of retention.
Also popcorning is an issue without a lid.

Pretty good profile for filter coffee.
The comparison would be against the Fellow Ode or Wilfa Uniform.
It outperforms both, so the small price bump checks out.
Also very good profile for espresso.
The most common comparison here is the Niche, but it’s a tough call.

Does the cup taste better than the Niche? For filter coffee? Yes. For espresso? YMMV.
But the experience of using the Niche is much more pleasing.
If you care more about the workflow, the Niche is still ahead.

The DF64 delivers in the cup, specially after a few tweaks.
If you don’t want to think about the grinder, it’s not for you.
Maybe a Lagom P64 would solve those pain points from the get go.
It’s a great choice for in-the-cup purist.
